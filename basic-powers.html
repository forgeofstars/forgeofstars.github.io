    Administer First Aid

      Manipulate
    Requirements
    You have healer’s tools

    You perform first aid on an adjacent creature with the dying condition. Attempt a Medicine roll with DC 15.

    Success
    The creature loses the dying condition (but remains downed)
    Critical Failure
    The creature’s dying condition increases by 1

    Aid

    You try to help your ally with a task. The task but be something you could reasonably help with, must involve a skill roll, and must be completed before the start of your next turn. You must explain to the GM exactly how you’re trying to help, and they determine whether you can Aid your ally. Attempt a skill roll (of a type decided by the GM) against DC 20.

    Critical Success
    You grant your ally a bonus to the roll equal to your Prof
    Success
    You grant your ally a +1 bonus to the roll
    Critical Failure
    Your ally takes a -1 penalty to the roll

    Delay

    Trigger
    Your turn begins

    You wait for the right moment to act. The rest of your turn doesn’t happen yet. Instead, you’re removed from the initiative order. You can return to the initative order as a power at the end of any other creature’s turn. This permanently changes your initiative to the new position.

    When you Delay, any persistent damage or other negative effects that normally occur during your turn occur immediately. Essentially, you can’t Delay to avoid negative consequences or to extend beneficial effects that would end on your turn.

    Disarm

      Attack
    Requirements
    You have at least one free hand, and your target is not more than one size larger than you

    You try to knock something out of an opponent’s grasp. Attempt a Brawling check against the opponent’s Reflex DC.

    Critical Success
    You knock the item out of the opponent’s grasp. It falls to the ground in the opponent’s space.
    Success
    You weaken your opponent’s grasp on the item. Until the start of that creature’s turn, attempts to Disarm the opponent of that item gain a +2 bonus, and the target takes a -2 penalty to Attack powers with the item or other rolls requiring a firm grasp on the item.
    Critical Failure
    You lose your balance and become flat-footed until the start of your next turn

    Drop Prone

      Move

    You fall prone.

    Escape

      Attack

    You attempt to escape from being grabbed, immobilized, or restrained. Choose one effect imposing any of these conditions on you. Attempt a Acrobatics, Athletics, or Brawling roll against the DC of the effect (such as the Brawling DC of a creature grabbing you, or the Spell DC of a spell effect).

    Critical Success
    As success, and you can then Stride up to 5 feet
    Success
    You get free and remove the grabbed, immobilized, and restrained conditions imposed by your chosen target
    Critical Failure
    You don’t get free, and you can’t attempt to Escape again this turn

    Force Open

      Attack

    You attempt to forcefully open a door, window, container, or heavy gate. Make an Athletics roll against the DC of the object.

    Critical Success
    You open the barrier and can avoid damaging it in the process
    Success
    You break the barrier open
    Critical Failure
    Your attempt jams the barrier shut, imposing a -2 penalty on future attempts to Force Open the barrier

    Grapple

      Attack
    Requirements
    You have at least one free hand, and your target is not more than one size larger than you

    You attempt to grab an opponent with your free hand. Attempt a Brawling roll against their Fortitude DC. You can also use this power to keep your hold a creature you already grabbed.

    Critical Success
    Your opponent is restrained until the end of your next turn, unless you move or your opponent succeeds on an Escape
    Success
    Your opponent is grabbed until the end of your next turn, unless you move or your opponent succeeds on an Escape
    Failure
    You fail to grab your opponent, and if you already had the opponent grabbed or restrained using Grapple, those conditions on that creature end
    Critical Failure
    As failure, and your target can either grab you (as if it had succeeded at using the Grapple power against you) or force you to fall and land prone

    Interact

      Manipulate

    You use your hand or hands to manipulate an object or the terrain. You can grab an unattended or stored object, open a door, or produce some similar effect. You might have to attempt a skill roll to determine if your Interact action was successful.

    Leap

      Move

    You take a careful, short jump. You can leap up to half your Speed horizontally, or up to a quarter of your Speed vertically. Jumping a greater distance requires using the Acrobatics skill.

    Point Out

      Auditory Manipulate Visual
    Requirements
    A creature you can see is hidden to one or more of your allies

    You indicate a creature that you can see to one or more allies, gesturing in a direction and describing the distance verbally. The target is no longer hidden to your allies.

    Ready

      Concentrate

    You prepare to use an action that will occur outside your turn. Choose a single power you can use, and designate a trigger. If the trigger occurs before the start of your turn, you can use the chosen power as a reaction.

    If you have MAP and your readied action is an Attack, your readied action takes the MAP you had at the end of your turn.

    Release

      Manipulate

    You release something you’re holding in your hand or hands. This might mean dropping an item, removing one hand from your weapon while continuing to hold it in another hand, releasing a rope suspending a chandelier, or performing a similar action.

    Seek

      Concentrate Secret

    You scan an area for signs of creatures or objects. If precision is necessary, select a 15-foot burst within line of sight. The GM attemps a single secret Perception roll for you can compares the result to the Stealth DCs of any hidden creatures or objects in the area.

    Success
    You observe the creature or object, making it no longer hidden to you

    Shove

      Attack
    Requirements
    You have at least one free hand, and your target is not more than one size larger than you

    You push an opponent away from you. Attempt a Brawling check against your opponent’s Fortitude DC.

    Critical Success
    You push your opponent up to 10 feet away from you. You can Stride after it, but you must move the same distance and in the same direction.
    Success
    You push your opponent back 5 feet. You can Stride after it, but you must move the same distance and in the same direction.
    Critical Failure
    You lose your balance, fall, and land prone

    Stand

      Move

    You stand up from prone.

    Step

      Move
    Requirements
    Your Speed is at least 10 feet

    You carefully move 5 feet. Unlike most types of movement, Step doesn’t trigger reactions that are triggered by Move actions or upon leaving or entering a square. You can’t Step into difficult terrain.

    Stride

      Move

    You move a distance up to your Speed.

    Strike

      Attack

    You attack with a weapon you’re wielding, targeting one creature within your reach (for a melee attack) or within range (for a ranged attack). Make an attack roll for the weapon you’re using, and compare the result to your target’s AC to determine the effect. See here for more information.

    Critical Success
    As success, but you deal double damage
    Success
    You deal damage according to the weapon you’re wielding

    Sustain

      Concentrate
    Requirements
    You have at least one spell active with a sustained duration

    Choose one spell with a sustained duration you have in effect. The duration of that spell continues until the end of your next turn. Some spells might have slightly different or expanded effects if you sustain them. If your Sustain power is disrupted, the spell immediately ends.

    Take Aim

      Concentrate
    Requirements
    You are wielding a ranged weapon

    The range penalty you take for attacking targets beyond your first range increment decreases to -2 for each range increment after the first (instead of -5). This applies only to the first attack you make before the end of your turn.

    Take Cover

    Requirements
    You are benefitting from cover or are prone

    If you are benefitting from cover, the cover’s bonus is doubled. Alternatively, if you are prone, you gain the benefits of standard cover against enemies at least 15 feet away from you. This lasts until you move from your current space, use an Attack power, become unconscious, or end this effect as a power.

    Talk

      Auditory Linguistic

    You speak a single sentence or so. Since Talk is a power, you can Talk as much as you want during combat, whether on your or another creature’s turn. If players try to misuse this by speaking for several minutes during a combat round, the GM should give Talk the following optional property:

    Frequency
    Once per round

    Trip

      Attack
    Requirements
    You have at least one free hand, and your target is not more than one size larger than you

    You try to knock an opponent to the ground. Attempt a Brawling check againt the target’s Reflex DC.

    Critical Success
    The target falls and lands prone and takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage
    Success
    The target falls and lands prone
    Critical Failure
    You lose your balance and fall and land prone
