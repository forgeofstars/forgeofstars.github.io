    Thematic Conflicts

    These conflicts drive much of the drama in the setting.

    Authority vs. Democracy

    Do the people need a firm hand to guide them, or is it best to let them decide how to govern themselves?

    The Empire, especially the Imperial military, is very authoritarian. The Empire believes that rule through fear is the best way to maintain public order.

    The Rebel Alliance is a strong supporter of democracy. They are governed by a council of rebel leaders, who represent groups of people and vote on what actions to take.

    Chaos vs. Order

    Is life best in a world where anything goes, or should everything be strictly managed and set in place?

    The crime syndicates embrace lives of chaos. The worlds they control are hives of scum and villainy where the daring and ambitious thrive while the common man is left behind.

    The Trade Federation exemplifies lives of order. Every part of a Federation employeeâ€™s life is standardized and restricted to ensure maximum efficiency.

    Progress vs. Tradition

    Should we take risks to change the world we live in, or would it be wiser to stick to the old ways?

    The arcanists believe in progressing scientific knowledge however possible. Their magical experiments tend to disregard any possible side effects on the mundane world.

    The Librarians uphold the traditional practice of magic. They guard against potential magical catastrophes by preventing the spread of dangerous arcane secrets.

    Ambition vs. Harmony

    Should we passionately strive towards an difficult goal, or should we accept the world as it is?

    The Humanitarian Church was founded to meet the ambitious goal of providing for all people. Members tend to be driven and committed to achieving the greater good.

    The Jedi Order pursues peacy and harmony throughout the galaxy. Members give up their material attachments so they can better see the spiritual connections between all things.

    Nature vs. Technology

    Should we preserve the natural world how it is, or should we improve on it through modern technology?

    The neoshamans worship the spirits of nature. They believe that harming nature means harming a spirit, an inherently evil act that should be avoided at all costs.

    The Cult Mechanicus worship the machine spirits. They believe that building technological devices honors these spirits, and want to cover the galaxy in machines.
